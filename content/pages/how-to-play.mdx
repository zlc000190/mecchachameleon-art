Meccha Chameleon is a multiplayer hide-and-seek game built around paint and visual camouflage. Hiders copy the colours and textures around them, choose a pose, and try to disappear into the map. Seekers inspect suspicious shapes, shadows, and colour mismatches before the round timer expires.

This beginner guide explains the match loop without making you memorise every map first.

## The basic match loop

1. Join quick play or create a private room for friends.
2. The game assigns you a Hider or Seeker role.
3. Hiders use the preparation time to choose a surface, paint their body, and settle into a believable pose.
4. Seekers enter after the preparation phase and search the room.
5. Hiders win by remaining undiscovered; Seekers win by finding the hidden players before time runs out.

## How to play as a Hider

Choose the surface before choosing the colour. A wall, curtain, bookshelf, tiled floor, or group of props gives you a clear visual target. Randomly painting first usually produces a colour that fits nowhere.

Match more than the dominant colour. A brick wall may be mostly red, but the grey mortar and dark shadow are what make your outline visible. Look for a main colour, a secondary colour, and the direction of the light.

Your silhouette matters as much as your paint. Turn your body so arms, legs, and head overlap nearby shapes. Busy areas are often safer than empty dark corners because the Seeker has more visual information to process.

Once you commit to a spot, stay still. Last-second movement attracts attention faster than a small colour error. Move only when the Seeker has clearly turned away or when your current position has already been checked.

## How to play as a Seeker

Scan the map in layers. Check obvious corners quickly, then look for broken patterns: a straight line that suddenly bends, a shadow pointing the wrong way, or a colour that is close but not quite right.

Change your viewing angle. Many hiding poses work from the front but fail from the side. Walk past suspicious objects, turn back, and compare their outline with the surrounding furniture.

Do not spend the entire round on one doubtful spot. Search high-probability areas first, remember anything suspicious, and return after covering the rest of the room.

## A simple first-round plan

- Play one short round before studying every mechanic.
- As a Hider, use one easy surface with two clear colours.
- Choose a compact pose and stop moving early.
- As a Seeker, search by room section instead of running randomly.
- After the round, open the [hiding spots guide](/hiding-spots) and compare your choice with the map examples.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Meccha Chameleon difficult for beginners?

The rules are simple. The difficult part is learning how colour, lighting, pose, and map routes work together. Start with obvious surfaces before attempting high-risk hiding spots.

### Should I hide in the darkest corner?

Not automatically. Seekers check dark corners first. A patterned or cluttered area with good paint and a believable pose is often stronger.

### Can I play with friends?

Use a private room when the selected game supports room creation, then share the room code. Check the game detail page because lobby support varies between browser games.
